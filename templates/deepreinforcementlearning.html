Deep reinforcement learning is a cutting-edge field that combines deep learning and reinforcement learning to train intelligent agents. Agents learn from interactions with an environment, receiving rewards or penalties based on their actions, and aim to maximize cumulative rewards over time. .

Deep neural networks enable agents to process complex inputs and make informed decisions. Key algorithms include Deep Q-Networks (DQN) and policy gradient methods.

Deep reinforcement learning has achieved impressive results in domains such as robotics, gaming, and natural language processing. Challenges include sample inefficiency, computational requirements, and generalization.

Overall, deep reinforcement learning shows great promise for enhancing AI capabilities in decision-making and complex environments.

Autonomous Drone Navigation

The primary objective of this project is to enable the drone's autonomous navigation capabilities, enabling it to reach its designated goal or destination swiftly and without any crashes.

Our project employs the powerful technique of Deep Reinforcement Learning with Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO). This approach leverages the capabilities of neural networks and reinforcement learning algorithms to enable the drone to autonomously navigate towards its goal or destination efficiently and effectively. By utilizing PPO, we can optimize the drone's policy, allowing it to learn from experience and make intelligent decisions in real-time, leading to improved performance and faster navigation.

Environment

In this project, a 2D environment was utilized and simulated using Unity Game Engine specifically UnityMLAgents package.

Observations

  • Drone X Position
  • Drone Y Position
  • Goal X Position
  • Goal Y Position
  • Drone X Velocity
  • Drone Y Velocity
  • Drone to Goal Distance
  • Drone to Goal Angle
  • Ray Preception

Actions

Continous Actions:

  • Left
  • Right

Discrete Actions:

  • 0 - Do Not Thrust and 1 - Thrust
  • 0 - Not Allowed to Move and 1 - Allowed to Move

Agent

The agent employed in this project is a Micro Aerial Vehicle (MAV) specifically designed for 2D environments.

MAVs are lightweight unmanned drones that are highly portable.

MAVs can be designed to carry out tasks autonomously or under remote human control.

Architecture

The architecture of the network with 128 hidden units and 3 layers.

The architecture also incorporates the use of GAIL (Generative Adversarial Imitation Learning) to enhance the training process. GAIL is a technique that leverages expert demonstrations to guide the learning of the agent, resulting in improved training efficiency. The GAIL reward signal in this architecture has a strength of 0.5 and a discount factor (gamma) of 0.6.

  • Learning Rate: 4.0e-4
  • Epsilon: 0.1
